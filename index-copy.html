In Aotearoa New Zealand, a public servant is any person who works for central or local government.

Public servants work all around Aotearoa New Zealand. About one in five jobs are in the public sector.

A skill unique to public servants is writing policy and so shaping the future of our country. But that’s not all public servants do! They work across a wide range of fields from health to justice, immigration to education, border security to conservation.

Yes, absolutely. Many people are drawn to being a public servant because they want to make a difference and contribute to creating a better New Zealand.

About 1 in 5 jobs in Aotearoa New Zealand are in the public sector. From working in customer-facing roles to developing policy, a career in the public sector can be exciting, challenging and rewarding.

There are many ways to navigate your public service career – whether you move between government agencies, across the public-private-NFP sectors, or gain experience overseas – the public sector needs diversity in its people and their experience.

No matter what part of the public sector you hope to work in, engaging with Hāpai Public can help.

We will help you deepen your understanding of the public sector, connect with other professionals and learn about future opportunities. You don’t need to be working in the public sector to join. Hāpai Public supports those from outside the public sector who are engaged in or interested in public service.

If you are interested in beginning a career in the public sector, there are many ways to start. One of the first steps you can take is to define what area of the public sector you would like to explore. Consider whether you want to work in front-line services or take a more administration-based approach.

Are you attracted to a particular area of work, like conservation, policing or education?

The public sector spans many roles, disciplines, agencies and professions. What binds them all together is the desire to work in service of the public and improve outcomes for all New Zealanders. Exploring the website of the Public Service Commission Te Kawa Mataaho is a good next step.
