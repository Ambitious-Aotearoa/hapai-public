Hāpai Public exists to support the public sector and those working in it

As the national body for public sector professionals, we have over 14,000 members from across the public, private and not-for-profit sectors. What ties us together is a passion for public service and a curiosity for exploring new ideas.

Public sector professionals are at the forefront of public service

They have a unique insight into what makes public sector policy and administration tick. As such, they need an independent space where they can come together to exchange knowledge, debate ideas and explore a deeper sense of purpose.

Hāpai Public provides a neutral, supportive space for sector exploration and critique

We provide a forum where members can connect with their peers to discuss and debate big issues, a platform where members can learn from industry leaders, a place where members can deepen their understanding of their sector, and a way for members to be at the front of ideas that promote innovation and positive sector change.

We go beyond helping members strengthen their individual capabilities

We support future good government by identifying and bridging gaps in the system, facilitate problem solving on sector-related issues and provide an authoritative voice for increasing public understanding of the work the Public Sector does. With Hāpai Public at their side, public sector professionals are better supported to shape a stronger sector.

1934

We were formed by public servants for public servants

For 90 years, we’ve been supporting public sector professionals to explore what it means to ‘do’ public service. Our story starts in 1934 when a group of public servants in Christchurch got together, moved by a desire to learn something of the broader background and common principles of public administration. Together, they formed the Canterbury Public Service Administration Society.

1936

Interest in exploring these ideas spread throughout New Zealand

Interest in exploring the work of public administration outside of the ordinary work of a department spread. Similar societies quickly cropped up in Wellington, Dunedin and Auckland. In 1936 these groups joined forces to become one national organisation called the New Zealand Institute of Public Administration. The name later changed to the Institute of Public Administration New Zealand or IPANZ for short.

2024

IPANZ became Hāpai Public, Institute of Public Professionals

In 2024, IPANZ adopted a new name: Hāpai Public, Institute of Public Professionals. Nearly ninety years on, Hāpai Public continues to hold a special place working alongside the New Zealand public sector and is Aotearoa’s leading professional organisation for public servants.

Our strategic direction is set and monitored by a governance board who are elected by you, our members. Our membership includes more than 120 organisations as well as individuals working within or closely with the public sector.

As an incorporated society, we’re governed by our constitution. Learn more about our strategic direction.
