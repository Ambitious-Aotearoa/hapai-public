About New Professionals

New Professionals is a great way to kick-start your career in the Public Sector. Joining the network makes it easy for you to form relationships across the public, private and not for profit sectors and learn from people who are good at what they do.

Who is New Professionals for?

Nau mai, piki mai! Our network is for all kaimahi working in, or with, the New Zealand public service, who are in their first ten years working and would like to know more about how the public sector works.

Most public sector organisations are members of IPANZ. If your organisation is a member, you are eligible to sign up to attend New Professional events and offerings for free and attend our training events at a discounted rate.

What we do

We plan and deliver the New Professionals programme. This involves delivering events, webinars, training seminars, and producing thought leadership pieces and articles for the Public Sector Journal.

New Professionals Events and Offerings

Meet The Chiefs breakfast seminars

At these events, chief executives (CEs) reflect on their careers – often sharing deep insights, helpful tips, and their perspective on what is or will be important. Coffee, breakfast, and the opportunity to quiz a CE – all provided.

Matariki Puanga in Wellington

We take the opportunity to connect, reflect on what Matariki Puanga means for us as New Professionals – and engage in deep kōrero following a Hautapu (dawn ceremony), with kai.

Networking

At these events, new professionals mix and mingle, form connections and get to know what different workplaces and roles are like. Members enjoy deepening their connections and connecting with likeminded people across the public, private and not-for-profit sectors.

Training

We offer introductory training courses including Public Sector 101 and Parliament in Practice. Public Sector 101 is an online course designed to provide you with a comprehensive introduction to the public sector in just 8 ½ hours over six sessions. We cover a lot of ground, and there are sessions on:

  • te tiriti o Waitangi
  • parliament, legislation and the constitution,
  • ministers, officials and the policy process
  • parliament's financial cycle, and
  • scrutiny and integrity.
