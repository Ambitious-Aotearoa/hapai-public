Why public services matter

The public sector exists to serve New Zealand. Learn why trust in the public services is so important.

What is the public sector?

The public sector refers to the parts of our country that are controlled by the government. It includes local government and all the central government and agencies responsible for providing public and regulatory services.

What is the role of the public sector?

The public sector's role is to provide policy advice and to fund or deliver public services, such as healthcare, education, public transport, and social welfare, as well as oversee regulations and maintain infrastructure.

Why is it important?

A thriving public sector is important for ensuring that citizens' needs are met, and public funds are managed responsibly. Well-designed services serve the public interest and support the country's overall well-being.

Why do we need a thriving public sector?

Public services play an important role in making a meaningful difference for New Zealanders. A thriving public sector is important because it:

Enables governments to effectively develop and implement policies

Governments need to be able to develop and implement their policies. The public sector provides the support and knowledge that elected politicians need to form policies and make informed decisions that address society's needs.

The public sector also ensures that once policies are established, they are actioned effectively. A stable public sector ensures that policies can continue smoothly from one government to the next, allowing long-term goals to be achieved without interruption.

Ensures high standards of governance and accountability

A strong public sector ensures high standards of governance and accountability by establishing fair and transparent frameworks for how decisions are made. Our public sector helps to uphold the law are ensures that all government actions are legal. These checks and balances ensure that government officials are always accountable and help to build trust with the general public.

Delivers high-quality and efficient public services

A strong public sector ensures that public resources are used wisely. It establishes rules and procedures for allocating funds, making sure that money is directed where it’s needed most and is spent on projects that benefit the public.

The oversight of the public sector helps to get the most value from public resources and ensures they contribute to achieving public goals. This also helps develop and implement policies that address challenges that will affect New Zealanders today and in the future.

Supports the Crown in its relationships with Māori

In New Zealand, our public service plays an important role in supporting the Crown relationship with Māori. A strong public sector is important for upholding Te Tiriti o Waitangi, the Treaty of Waitangi and improving services and outcomes for Māori.

Facilitates active citizenship

Transparency in the public sector helps build trust, making people feel that their voices matter and that their participation in processes like voting, has a real impact. This is important as active citizens are more likely to vote, participate in public consultations, and contribute to their communities.

When public sector professionals are well-supported, they have the resources, training, and knowledge to carry out their roles efficiently and ethically. This helps them identify and address issues, operate with neutrality, and ensures more transparency within our government.
