Our work supports a thriving Public Sector

We promote a thriving Public Sector by providing fertile ground for connecting people and ideas. We serve our members by building a community of learning and practice about public services. Constant over time has been our focus on the activity of public services, on delivery for the people, by the people.

We act as an independent voice

We are an independent organisation. We serve as an independent voice and work towards advancing good government practices, fostering collaboration, enhancing resilience within the sector and deepening public understanding of the work public servants do.

Our objective is to create a stronger Public Sector for all

Building a more effective Public Sector starts with supporting the people working within it. We aim to strengthen the Public Sector by ensuring that Public Sector professionals are highly engaged, that agencies are well connected, and that public understanding remains strong.

Our Kaupapa – How we do it

Extend your reach through networking

We run networking events so you can extend your reach across the Public Sector and into the private and not-for-profit sectors. Stronger ties across the sector help members build their confidence, strengthen relationships and gain a different perspective on work across the sector. Our New Professionals network is specifically designed to help those who are in the first ten years of their career develop personal networks while deepening their understanding of the Public Sector.

Stay up to date with insights and intel

We make it easy for members to stay up to date with the latest thought leadership, news and insights from the sector’s leading minds. Membership gives you access to the quarterly Public Sector Journal which contains opinion and analysis on current sector issues, while our 3-weekly newsletter explores a wide range of the latest developments.

Build your capabilities with events and training

Through events and training, members can build their capabilities at every level. Our event calendar includes online webinars, discounted foundation training courses, free event videos, the Public Sector Conference and access to the Aspiring Senior Leaders course. You’ll also enjoy free face-to-face events and networking opportunities in Whanganui-a-Tara Wellington and Tāmaki Makaurau Auckland.

Work in support of good government

In support of good government members can take part in roundtables with senior professionals tackling the significant issues facing the Public Sector as well as workshops on critical issues facing the sector. From time to time, we make submissions on behalf on the sector that benefits those working within it.

How does Hāpai Public differ from the Public Service Commission and the PSA?

The Commission leads the public service, ensuring that the public service system is focused on delivering the outcomes and services that New Zealanders want, need and expect while maintaining high levels of trust and confidence. The Commission provides leadership and oversight of the public service, ensuring that it carries out its purpose. It is a Public Sector agency.

The Public Service Association is New Zealand’s largest trade union, representing the interests of over 90,000 workers in the public sector. They aim to influence the political, economic, industrial and social environments in the interests of the membership of the PSA.

Hāpai Public is a not-for-profit membership-based organisation. Our purpose is to promote a strong Public Sector – we do this by working alongside the Public Sector, providing a community of learning and practice about public service. We are independent: we sit outside the Public Sector.
